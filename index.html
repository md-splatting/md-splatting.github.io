MD-Splatting: Learning Metric Deformation from 4D Gaussians in Highly Deformable Scenes

Bardienus P. Duisterhof1, Mandi Zhao2, Yunchao Yao1, Jia-Wei Liu3
Mike Zheng Shou3, Shuran Song2, Jeffrey Ichnowski1
1Carnegie Mellon University, 2Stanford University, 2National University of Singapore
Paper arXiv Code Data

Abstract

Accurate 3D tracking in highly deformable scenes with occlusions and shadows can facilitate new applications in robotics, augmented reality, and generative AI. However, tracking under these conditions is extremely challenging due to the ambiguity that arises with large deformations, shadows, and occlusions. We introduce MD-Splatting, an approach for simultaneous 3D tracking and novel view synthesis, trained on video captures of a dynamic scene from various camera poses. MD-Splatting builds on re- cent advances in Gaussian splatting and learns a defor- mation function to project canonical Gaussians into metric space. The deformation function uses a canonical neural voxel encoding and a multi-layer perception (MLP) to infer Gaussian position, rotation, and a shadow scalar. When training MD-Splatting, we enforce physics-inspired regu- larization terms based on local rigidity, conservation of mo- mentum, and isometry, which lead to a high-capacity met- ric deformation function and a canonical Gaussian repre- sentation. MD-Splatting achieves high-quality 3D tracking on highly deformable scenes with shadows and occlusions. Compared to state-of-the-art, we improve 3D tracking by an average of 16.7 %, while simultaneously achieving high- quality novel view synthesis

Method

MD-Splatting maps a set of Gausians with canonical properties to metric space using a deformation function F. The deformation function takes in the position of a Gaussian (x, y, z) and a queried timestamp t, to infer shadow s, rotation r' and metric position (x', y', z'). During training, the metric positions and rotations are used to regularize the deformation function.
